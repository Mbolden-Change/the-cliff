      Where these numbers come from
      
These stories run on mBOLDen Change's benefits-cliff engine — a verified, parameter-driven model of California safety-net interactions: CalWORKs (ACL 21-130, ACL 24-55), CalFresh (7 CFR 273.9–273.11, ACIN I-46-25), the Housing Choice Voucher rent formula (24 CFR 5.628, FY2026 FMR schedule), Medi-Cal / Covered California (ACWDL 26-01), and federal and state tax credits for TY2025 (Rev. Proc. 2024-40; FTB CalEITC tables). Program amounts, rent shares, clawbacks, and the tax-refund pulse are computed at play time from those parameters.

      
Maya's household is Scenario S3 in our Benefit Cliff Scenario Library; Dani's is Scenario S2 (the CalFresh 200% gross-income screen); Jay's is Scenario S8 (the adult Medi-Cal boundary at 138% FPL); Rio's applies the guaranteed-income countability rules (WIC 11157(e); ACL 23-60 non-governmental-funds test; IRC 102) to a single-adult household. The characters are fictional; their math is not. Household spending on food and essentials is held constant so that only the rules move.

      
Story grounding: Rio's circumstances follow the CalYOUTH study (Chapin Hall, University of Chicago), which finds more than one-third of California youth who age out of foster care experience homelessness and over 40% couch-surf; the pilot mirrors the County of Santa Clara's guaranteed basic income pilot for transition-age foster youth ($1,000/month, launched 2020 — the first in the nation). Rio's non-enrollment is typical: the GAO estimates a majority of students eligible for SNAP never receive it, and only about one in five homeless California community college students gets CalFresh. Jay's enrollment reflects the ACA adult expansion (Medi-Cal for adults 19–64 under 138% FPL). The Reyes household is Scenario S1 in the library — the children's Medi-Cal boundary at 266% FPL, set outside the three counties (SF, San Mateo, Santa Clara) whose C-CHIP bridge would soften it; the coverage-replacement valuation there carries the assumption-labeled utilization parameters documented in the engine. Cast representation reflects USDA SNAP household characteristics data (FY2023), in which white participants are the largest racial group nationally (35.4%).
